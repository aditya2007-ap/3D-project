SVJ Performance Engineering

An in-depth look at the cutting-edge systems and advanced aerodynamics that allow the Lamborghini Aventador SVJ to conquer the race track.

V12 Powerhouse

The Concept / Meaning

A naturally aspirated, high-revving 6.5-liter V12 internal combustion engine representing the absolute pinnacle of traditional mechanical power.

What It Does / Functionality

It converts high-octane fuel into 770 CV (770 HP) and 720 Nm of raw torque without the delay of turbochargers. It breathes in massive volumes of air through optimized intake runners, driving the pistons to an extreme 8,500 RPM to launch the car from 0-100 km/h in 2.8 seconds and unleash an exhaust note measuring 115 dB.

Specifications
770 CV @ 8,500 RPM Peak Power Output
6.5 Liters (6,498 cc) Engine Displacement
720 Nm @ 6,750 RPM Maximum Torque

LDVI Brain

The Concept / Meaning

Lamborghini Dinamica Veicolo Integrata (Lamborghini Integrated Vehicle Dynamics)—the centralized electronic brain coordinating the car’s active chassis systems.

What It Does / Functionality

It monitors driving inputs (steering angle, throttle pressure, braking force) and lateral/longitudinal G-forces at a rate of 50 times per second. By anticipating the driver’s intentions, it instantly coordinates and adjusts the settings of the active aerodynamics, magnetic suspension, all-wheel-drive system, and rear-wheel steering to ensure optimal traction and cornering safety.

Specifications
Feed-Forward Control Logic Architecture
50 Hz (Every 20ms) Sensor Monitoring Rate
Real-time Integration Execution Latency

Active Aero (ALA 2.0)

The Concept / Meaning

Aerodinamica Lamborghini Attiva (Lamborghini Active Aerodynamics) 2.0—a patented active system that varies aerodynamic configuration based on speed and cornering force.

What It Does / Functionality

It uses electric actuators to open or close air flaps in the front splitter and on the engine cover in under 500 milliseconds. Under heavy braking and cornering, flaps close to maximize downforce (+700% compared to standard SV). In straightaways, flaps open to route airflow under the body and wing, stalling the spoiler to minimize drag and maximize top speed.

Specifications
< 500 milliseconds Flap Activation Time
+700% Downforce Max Cornering Load
Aero Vectoring Left/Right Load Shifting

HMI Cockpit

The Concept / Meaning

Human-Machine Interface—the digital instrument cluster and center console display dedicated to vehicle management and tracking.

What It Does / Functionality

It serves as the driver's telemetry control room. It displays real-time track maps, registers lap times via integrated GPS, and monitors the active flaps of the ALA 2.0 system. It also allows configuration of the custom "Ego" driving mode, letting the driver mix-and-match steering, traction, and suspension firmness parameters independently.

Specifications
8.4" Touchscreen Infotainment Display
Dual-Camera System Telemetry Logging
Strada/Sport/Corsa/Ego Dynamic Drive Modes

All-Wheel Steering

The Concept / Meaning

Lamborghini Rear-Wheel Steering (LRS)—an active system that mechanically alters the steering angle of the rear axle.

What It Does / Functionality

At low speeds, LRS steers the rear wheels in the opposite direction of the front wheels (up to +/- 3°), virtualizing a shorter wheelbase to improve tight cornering agility. At high speeds, it steers the rear wheels in parallel with the front axle, virtualizing a longer wheelbase to ensure rock-solid stability during lane changes and fast bends.

Specifications
Up to +/- 3 Degrees Max Steering Angle
Virtual Wheelbase Adjustment Axle Tuning Logic
10.5 meters Minimum Turn Radius
